Executive Summary

This committee recommends a tiered investment strategy in Texas, led by Fort Worth, followed by Frisco and San Antonio. Fort Worth is ranked first for its superior balance of strong, sustainable growth, large market scale, and reasonable affordability, presenting the most durable risk-adjusted return profile. Frisco is ranked second as a high-conviction growth play, driven by its market-leading population and income momentum, despite significant valuation risks. Ranking Logic

The final ranking integrates a quantitative scoring model with a qualitative committee overlay. The committee, comprising Growth, Risk, Rental Demand, and Contrarian perspectives, weighed the merits of each city's profile. Fort Worth's top rank was secured by its consensus appeal; it satisfied the Rental Demand Analyst's need for scale, the Risk Officer's preference for balance, and the Contrarian's thesis for affordable core cities. Frisco's explosive growth metrics, championed by the Growth and Appreciation Strategist, propelled it to the second position, overriding concerns about its high valuation. San Antonio's strong fundamentals in affordability, yield, and renter concentration earned it the third spot, representing a more conservative, cash-flow-oriented investment.
